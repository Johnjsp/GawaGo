INTRODUCTION

         The employment of domestic helpers and skilled workers in Tayabas City, Quezon has

long  depended      on   informal   arrangements       such   as  personal    referrals  and   word-of-mouth

recommendations, leaving both households and workers without a reliable, transparent, and

organized means       of  connecting    with   one another. According        to the Philippine Institute for

Development       Studies     (PIDS,   2022),    digital   labor    platforms     remain    limited    in  many

semi-urban     communities       in  the   Philippines,    resulting   in  restricted    access   to  organized

employment       opportunities     and   reliable  hiring   systems.   This  absence of a structured local

employment       platform     has   contributed      to   persistent    challenges     including    information

asymmetry, lack      of  worker verification,     and   limited access to job opportunities. To address

these   gaps,  the   researchers    propose     the  development of       a  web   and mobile employment

system   that will centralize and digitize the hiring process for households, domestic helpers,

and   skilled  workers     in Tayabas     City, incorporating     smart    matching,     proximity-aware      job

allocation,   and    reputation     scoring    to   ensure    an   inclusive,   efficient,   and    trustworthy

employment ecosystem for the community.


Project Context
         Digital labor platforms have significantly transformed the labor market, particularly in

the way workers and employers connect. According to the International Labour Organization

(ILO, 2021), the number of digital labor platforms worldwide has increased fivefold over the

past decade. In developing countries such as the Philippines, these platforms are considered

a promising     source   of  employment       opportunities,     enabling    workers    —   including   women,

young   people, and persons with disabilities — to             access income-generating          work.   Despite

this global trend, localized employment            platforms    that  address the specific socioeconomic

conditions    of  municipalities     and   cities  in  the  Philippines    remain     limited,  particularly    in


<!-- Page 4 -->

semi-urban areas such as Tayabas City.

         One   of  the  key   issues   motivating    this  study   is the continued reliance on informal

hiring  practices within local communities           in the Philippines.     Soriano (2021) noted that the

Philippine    labor   market    is  largely   characterized     by  informal    employment        arrangements

where     workers     and    employers      depend     on    personal     referrals,   social   networks,     and

word-of-mouth to find opportunities. This situation is common in community-based services

such as   domestic     work and     household skilled labor, where formal recruitment systems are

limited.  Although     these   practices   are familiar   in  many    communities, they often         result in a

lack  of  standardized     compensation,       worker    verification,    and   job  security.   The   proposed

system   aims   to  address    this gap   by providing a structured and transparent digital platform

for connecting households and workers in Tayabas.

         Trust  and accountability      are  also  critical factors in    online labor markets. A study by

Hesse    and   Teubner     (2023),   published     in  Electronic    Markets,    examined      how    reputation

signals  such   as  ratings   and   reviews from completed transactions influence                user  trust and

hiring  decisions    on   gig   economy     platforms.     The   study   found    that  workers     with   higher

aggregated ratings are more frequently hired and are generally perceived as more reliable.

These    findings   support     the  inclusion    of  a  reputation     scoring    system    in  the   proposed

platform,    allowing   households      to  rate   workers    after  job  completion      while   also  enabling

workers to rate households in order to promote fairness and accountability on both sides.

         The  accessibility of digital services across devices has also been                identified   as a key

factor in  the adoption of employment platforms. The development of a mobile application

component       alongside     the   web    platform     ensures     that   users    can   access    the   system

conveniently through their smartphones, which are among the most widely used devices in

the Philippines. According        to the   Philippine Statistics    Authority (PSA,      2023), mobile phone

ownership continues to rise even in semi-urban and rural areas, making mobile applications

a practical   and   inclusive  channel for delivering       digital  employment       services to    households

and workers in Tayabas City.

         This study aims to      address    these   gaps by    developing     a centralized web and mobile

Scope and Limitations of the Study


<!-- Page 7 -->

This  study   focuses   on   the  development       of a  web   and mobile employment           system
designed    to connect households with domestic helpers and skilled workers within Tayabas
City, Quezon.     The   system    aims   to  provide    an  organized     and   accessible    platform    where
households      can   easily   find   workers    while    workers    can   access    available    employment
opportunities.    The    intended    users   of  the  system    include    households,     domestic     helpers,
skilled  workers,     and    system    administrators      who    are   responsible     for  monitoring      and
managing     the   platform.     The   development       and   implementation        of  the  system     will  be
conducted within the duration of the research period.

         The  system will include several main components to support employment matching

and platform     management. These include a Household Dashboard for posting job requests

and   managing     worker    applications,    a  Worker    Profile   and  Dashboard      where    workers    can

create  accounts,    present    their skills, and apply     for  jobs, and   a  Job Listing   and Application

section  where    available   job  opportunities are displayed. An          Administrative     Dashboard     will

allow administrators to monitor system activities, verify user accounts, and manage services.

The   platform     will   also   include    an   Employment        Analytics    Dashboard       that   presents

summarized      information     such as   job  postings, completed       services,   verified  users,   and the

distribution   of  services   across   barangays     in Tayabas    City.  To  improve    efficiency   and   user

experience,     the   system     will  include    features    such    as   Smart    Matching      and   Worker

Recommendation,        which    suggests    workers    based   on   skills, availability,  verification status,

and approximate distance from the job location. A Reputation and Review System will allow

users  to  provide    ratings  and   feedback     after  job  completion. The      system    will also provide

multi-channel notifications through email and in-system alerts. Additional features include a

Distance   Indicator    that  shows    the approximate distance         between the      worker    and   the job

site, service   rate   transparency     in  worker    profiles,  user   verification   monitoring,    barangay

employment       distribution    analytics,    and   Role-Based      Access    Control   to   manage     system

security. The platform is developed as a mobile application using Flutter, supported by a
Django backend that provides shared API endpoints for user accounts, job postings, hiring
requests, notifications, verification, reviews, and analytics. Through the mobile application,
households and workers can manage accounts, view job listings, and receive notifications
conveniently on their mobile devices.


         However, the      system    has  several limitations.     The platform     will  only  be available to
users within Tayabas City, Quezon. Most system features require internet connectivity, which
may affect users with limited access to the internet. The distance indicator will show
the approximate straight-line distance between the worker's registered location and the job
site. It will not calculate actual travel routes or travel time.
User verification will depend on administrative review of submitted identification documents
and will not be directly connected to government databases. In terms of payment handling, the system does not include built-in payment
processing     or  online    transactions.     All  payments     will  be   arranged     and    settled   directly
between     the household      and the    worker outside      the platform. The system only serves as a
medium for job matching and communication, and it does not monitor, record, or guarantee
any financial transactions between users. In addition, the effectiveness of the platform relies
on  the  active   participation    of  users in   applying for jobs      and providing honest        ratings and
feedback. The service rates shown in worker profiles are only reference values, and the final
hiring  decision and     pricing  will  still depend on the agreement between the household and
the worker.

Theoretical Framework

         The   development        of   digital   employment        platforms     has   become      increasingly
important     in   improving     the    way    job   seekers    and    employers      connect     within    local
communities.      Traditional    hiring  practices    in  many     areas   still rely on   informal    referrals,
word-of-mouth recommendations, and personal networks, which often result in limited job
opportunities      and    inefficient    matching      between       workers     and    employers.      Modern
information    systems    provide    technological solutions       that can address these challenges           by
organizing   employment information,           facilitating communication,        and improving the overall
hiring  process.    Web     platforms,    in  particular,    enable    users   to  access    services   through
internet-enabled      devices,    allowing    job   seekers    to  find  employment        opportunities     and
employers    to  identify qualified workers       more efficiently.     These   systems may also integrate
intelligent   features    such   as  automated       matching,    reputation     tracking,    and   notification
services   to   enhance      user   experience     and    system    reliability.  The   integration     of  such
technologies     can  significantly    improve    transparency,     efficiency,   and  trust   in employment
platforms,    particularly   in  local   communities      where    organized     labor   systems    are  limited
(Laudon & Laudon, 2022).

         According    to  Davis   (1989),   the  Technology     Acceptance      Model    (TAM) explains how
users  come    to  accept    and   utilize a  new   technology.     The   model    suggests    that  two   major
factors  influence    technology     adoption:     perceived    usefulness     and   perceived    ease   of  use.


<!-- Page 11 -->

Perceived usefulness refers to the degree to which a person believes that using a system will
enhance     job  performance,      while   perceived     ease   of  use  refers   to  the  degree    to  which    a
person believes that the system will be free from effort. Systems that are easy to understand
and   beneficial   to  users   are   more    likely  to  be  accepted     and   adopted.    In  relation   to  the
proposed     system,    the   Technology      Acceptance       Model     supports    the   development       of   a
user-friendly web and mobile employment platform that allows households and workers to
easily navigate the system. The inclusion of simple registration processes, intuitive interfaces
for both   web    and mobile, and email notifications helps ensure that users can interact with
the platform effectively across different devices.

         Blau (1964) introduced the Social Exchange Theory (SET), which explains that human
interactions are based       on the    exchange    of benefits    between     individuals. According       to this
theory,   relationships    are   formed    and    maintained     through     trust,  reciprocity,   and   mutual
benefits. In many social systems, individuals evaluate interactions based on the rewards and
costs associated with them. In digital platforms, trust is often established through feedback
mechanisms       and   repeated      interactions    among     users.   In  the   context    of  the   proposed
employment      system,    Social Exchange Theory supports            the implementation of a reputation
scoring and feedback mechanism. Through this feature, households can rate workers based
on   their  service    quality,   while    workers    can   also   evaluate    households      based    on   their
experience.     This   reciprocal    feedback      process    helps    create    accountability,     encourages
responsible behavior, and gradually builds trust among users within the platform.

         The  Diffusion    of Innovations Theory (DOI) proposed             by Rogers (1962) explains how
new   technologies     and   innovations     spread    within   a  social  system    over  time. According      to
Rogers (1962), the adoption of innovations is influenced by several factors, including relative
advantage,      compatibility     with    existing    practices,     simplicity    of   use,   trialability,  and
observability.   These factors     determine      how quickly individuals       or communities       adopt    new
technologies.     In  relation   to  the   proposed      system,    the   Diffusion   of  Innovations     Theory
supports the design of a platform that is compatible with existing technological habits of the
community. The       development of       a  mobile    application   component       ensures that users who
primarily rely    on  smartphones — which are widely accessible in Tayabas City — can adopt
and sustain usage of the platform more easily, thereby increasing the likelihood of successful
diffusion within the local community.


<!-- Page 12 -->

The   proposed      system     is also   supported      by  the   Job   Search    Theory,    originally
introduced    by  Stigler (1961) and later expanded by Diamond (1982) and Mortensen (1986)
as part   of  the  Search and     Matching Theory in        labor economics. This theory         explains    how
workers and     employers     search for each other in        labor markets where information is often
incomplete     or  imperfect.     Job  seekers    typically  spend    time   and    resources    searching    for
suitable   employment        opportunities,     while    employers     invest   effort   in  finding   qualified
candidates.    These    search   processes     can   result  in  delays,   inefficiencies,   and   mismatches
between     job  requirements      and worker     qualifications.    In relation to   this  study,  Job Search
Theory    supports    the   implementation        of  the   smart   matching     feature    of  the   proposed
employment system. By automatically recommending workers to households based on skills,
availability, verification status, and location, the system reduces the time and effort required
for  both   parties   to   find  suitable   employment        opportunities.     This  automated      matching
process   helps   improve efficiency      in the  local  labor market and provides a more structured
and organized employment connection within the Tayabas community.

Technical Terms

Distance    Indicator    –  A  measurement        that   shows    the   geographic     distance   between      two

routing services, to help households assess worker proximity and travel feasibility.

Email Notification – An electronic message sent through an email service to inform users of

important events and updates. In this study, it refers to automated email alerts sent by the

platform    to  inform   users   about    important     events   such as    job  alerts, application updates,

confirmations, and feedback reminders.

Employment         Analytics      Dashboard       –    A    digital    interface     that    visually    presents
employment-related         data    and   statistics.   In  this  study,   it  refers   to   the   administrative
dashboard that      displays information       such   as job   activity, verified users, geographic service
distribution    across   barangays,      and   service   rate   summaries      to   support    monitoring      and
decision-making.

Mobile    Application     –  A  software    application    designed     to  run  on   mobile    devices   such as

smartphones      and tablets.    In  this study, it refers to the mobile component of the proposed

platform that allows households and workers in Tayabas City to access employment features,

manage their accounts, and receive notifications through their mobile devices.

Smart    Matching      –  An    automated      process     that   recommends        or  pairs   users   based     on
predefined     criteria.  In  this  study,   it refers   to  the   system    feature    that   suggests    suitable
workers    to  households      based    on factors such as      skills, availability, verification status, and
geographic proximity to the job location.

Verification – The process of confirming the authenticity or validity of a person’s identity or

workers to receive a verification badge and higher priority in smart matching results.

Web    Employment        System    –  A  software     application    that  can   be accessed through a web
browser    using   an  internet connection. In this study, it refers to the proposed platform that
allows   households,     domestic     helpers, and     skilled  workers in     Tayabas City     to interact, post
jobs, apply for work, and manage employment activities online.


Operational Terms

Default Rating - A system-assigned neutral rating automatically given when a household does

Domestic Helper – An individual employed to perform household-related tasks and services

requests and hire domestic helpers or skilled workers through the system.

Job  Posting –    An   announcement or listing of available work               opportunities. In this study, it
refers to a job listing created by a household on the platform specifying the type of service
needed, required skills, schedule, location, and offered service rate.

Reputation Scoring        System –     A  method     used   to evaluate credibility or performance based

in a particular field. In this study, it refers to a registered user of the platform who provides

Employment Platforms and Digital Recruitment Systems

         The  rapid growth of digital labor platforms has fundamentally transformed the way

workers    and   employers      connect,    particularly   in  developing     countries.    The   International

Labour Organization (ILO, 2021) in its flagship report, World Employment and Social Outlook

2021: The Role of Digital Labour Platforms in Transforming the World of Work, documented

how    the  number     of  digital  labor   platforms     worldwide     increased    fivefold   over   the   past

decade. The report highlighted that in           developing countries such as the Philippines, digital

employment        platforms      are   considered      a   significant     source    of    income-generating

opportunities for marginalized groups including women, youth, and persons with disabilities.

Despite   this  global   growth,    the   ILO  emphasized      that   localized   platforms    addressing     the

socioeconomic       conditions     of  municipalities     and   semi-urban      areas    remain    insufficient,

particularly in communities where informal hiring arrangements continue to dominate.

In the   Philippine    context,   Soriano    (2021)   examined     digital  labor   brokerage    in  the

Philippines    through     a  four-year    digital   ethnography      published     in  Media     International

Australia. The study found that the Philippine labor market remains largely characterized by

informal   employment        arrangements      where     workers    and   employers     depend     on  personal

referrals, social   networks, and      word-of-mouth       to find job opportunities.       Soriano observed

that   while    digital   platforms     are    increasingly     accessible    to   Filipino   workers,     many

communities      —   particularly   those   outside    Metro    Manila    —   continue    to  rely on informal

hiring structures that lack standardization, worker verification, and employment security.

         At-Your-Service, a mobile application developed for skilled workers in the Philippines

and presented      at an  IEEE conference (Basbas et al., 2019), was designed to connect skilled

workers with employers through a digital platform that improved on traditional manpower

agency processes. The system allowed workers to register profiles and enabled employers to

post   available     jobs,   providing     a  structured     alternative     to   informal    hiring    in  local

communities.

         LocalLink, a mobile-based        application    for  local services   in barangay communities in

the Philippines, was developed          and evaluated with 55 participants (Supo et al., 2024). The

platform    aimed     to  connect    service    providers    and   users   within    localized   communities,

formalize   the informal economy, and enhance marketplace transparency. Evaluation results

showed a grand mean rating of 4.87, indicating that the platform effectively met user needs

and improved community connectivity.


Smart Matching and Recommendation Systems in Employment Platforms

         Möhlmann,      Zalmanson,      Henfridsson,    and Gregory      (2021)   published a study in       MIS

Quarterly    examining    algorithmic     management       on   online   labor platforms. The       researchers


<!-- Page 20 -->

found that    platforms    use   algorithms along two dimensions            — matching and        control   —   to

assign  work to    available   workers    in  real time based on skills, availability, and location. The

study,   which     drew     from    a   qualitative    examination       of   Uber's    algorithmic     systems,

demonstrated       that  algorithmic     matching    reduces     the  need   for  manual     coordination     and

enables    platforms    to  efficiently   process    large  volumes     of  job   assignments.     The   findings

emphasize that proximity-aware algorithmic matching is a practical and scalable solution for

employment platforms serving local communities where manual job coordination would be

inefficient.

         Nikolaou     (2021),   in  an   article  published     in  The   Spanish     Journal   of  Psychology,

reviewed how modern recruitment practices increasingly rely on automated technologies to

identify   qualified   candidates.     The   study    explained    that   smart    recruitment     technologies

enable   organizations     to screen    and match     candidates     more   effectively through automated

processes, reducing the workload of recruiters while improving the accuracy of job matching

results.  Nikolaou     emphasized      that   technology-driven       matching      systems    are   particularly

valuable   in  contexts   where    large volumes of       applications need to       be efficiently processed

and paired with suitable job opportunities.

         The Smart Employment Platform, a system presented in the International Journal of

Multidisciplinary     Research    (2025),    implemented       a  content-based      filtering  algorithm     that

recommends       jobs   to  workers    based    on   skills, location,   and   type   of  work.   The   platform

incorporated     proximity-based      job  allocation    alongside    employer     verification   and a secure

transaction    model,   demonstrating how intelligent matching and location awareness can be

integrated into a single employment system for blue-collar and skilled workers.

         Sanninganavar      et  al. (2021)   proposed     a digital  skill-based job-matching system that

prioritizes   practical    abilities   over    formal     qualifications,    featuring     structured     worker


<!-- Page 21 -->

registration,   detailed    skill profiling,  employer      job  postings,    and   an  automated      matching

engine.   The   system    was    evaluated    in  both   rural   and   urban   contexts    and   was   found    to

improve    worker    visibility  and   reduce    unemployment         by  efficiently   matching     workers    to

available job opportunities based on skills and proximity.


Reputation and Rating Systems in Online Service Platforms

         Hesse,   Teubner,     and   Adam     (2023),   in  a   study   published     in  Electronic   Markets,

examined how       reputation signals influence user          trust  and  hiring   decisions   in gig economy

platforms.    Using   an   online   experiment      involving   180   actual   clients   of five  gig  economy

platforms, the researchers found that workers with higher aggregated ratings are hired more

frequently    and    are   generally     perceived     as  more     reliable.   The   study    concluded     that

reputation systems based on verified completed transactions are among the most powerful

tools  for  establishing    trust   in digital  labor   markets,     supporting    the   value   of  integrating

mutual reputation scoring in employment platforms where both parties can rate each other.

         Dellarocas    (2003),    in  a  widely    cited   article  published     in  Management        Science,

examined the design and implications of online reputation mechanisms. The study explained

that  feedback     mechanisms       encourage     service   providers    to  maintain    high   service   quality

because    their  reputation directly      affects future    opportunities. Dellarocas emphasized that

reputation     systems     transform      informal     word-of-mouth        trust   into   a   scalable    digital

mechanism      that  helps   users make informed decisions when selecting                 service providers in

online environments.

         Resnick   and   Zeckhauser     (2002),   through    an  empirical analysis of      eBay's   reputation

system     published     in   The    Economics       of   the   Internet     and    E-Commerce        (Elsevier),

demonstrated       that   reputation     profiles   are   predictive    of  future   performance       and   that


<!-- Page 22 -->

feedback    mechanisms       encourage     trustworthy     behavior     between     buyers   and   sellers.  The

study  found    that  reputation     systems    are  effective at reducing      uncertainty    and promoting

cooperation    between     parties   who are unfamiliar       with   each  other   — a    key  requirement in

community-based employment platforms where households and workers may have no prior

relationship.

         The   LocalLink   platform    (Supo    et  al., 2024)   also  integrated    a  feedback    and   rating

component      that   allowed    users  to  evaluate    service   providers    after  each   transaction.    The

results  showed     that  the  reputation     feature   improved     transparency     and   allowed    users   to

more easily identify reliable service providers based on accumulated community feedback.


Employment Analytics and Monitoring Dashboards in Digital Platforms

         Aggarwal (2016), in the textbook Recommender Systems: The Textbook published by

Springer, discussed how        data  analytics and     monitoring tools within digital platforms           allow

administrators     to  examine     patterns    in  user   activity,  evaluate    system    performance,      and

generate    actionable    insights.  Aggarwal     explained    that   data  visualization    within   platforms

transforms     raw    system    data    into   meaningful     summaries       that   support     the   effective

management       and    continuous     improvement       of  digital  services   —   including    employment

platforms where monitoring job activity, user engagement, and service distribution is critical

for platform governance.

         The  Institute   for Labor   Studies   of  the Department of Labor and Employment in the

Philippines (ILS-DOLE, 2023), in its research report Facilitating Employment Opportunities in

the Digital Space,     studied the activities and services of Digital Labor Market Intermediaries

(DLMIs)   in  the  Philippines.    The  report    found   that data-driven monitoring         of  employment

activity  —   including   job postings, registered       users, and    job  vacancies — was       essential   for


<!-- Page 23 -->

understanding      employment       patterns   in  local  communities      and   for  informing labor     policy.

The study highlighted that platforms which maintain structured employment data are better

positioned to serve the workforce planning needs of communities.

         RecruitScope,     a  visual  analytics   system    developed     by   researchers    from   Huazhong

University    of  Science    and    Technology     (Wang     et   al., 2025),    was   designed     to  support

multidimensional       exploration     of  recruitment     data   for   job  seekers,    employers,     and   HR

specialists.  The    system    used   coordinated      visualizations    to  enable    users   to  analyze    job

positions   and salary patterns from multiple perspectives, identify industry dynamics at the

macro level, and discover high-demand roles at the micro level. Case studies demonstrated

that  the  platform    successfully    revealed    regional   salary   distribution   patterns    and   industry

growth trajectories through its analytics dashboard.

         The   Smart     Employment        Platform     (2025)    also   incorporated      an   administrative

monitoring    component that allowed          platform managers to track job postings, applications,

and employer activity in real time. The monitoring dashboard provided administrators with

an   overview      of   platform     usage    and    employment        outcomes,       enabling     data-driven

management decisions and continuous improvement of the system.

applications    designed     with   intuitive   navigation,     responsive     layouts,   and   context-aware

Synthesis

         The reviewed literature and systems presented in this chapter include both local and

international     sources    related    to  digital   employment        platforms,    smart     matching     and

proximity-aware       job   allocation,   reputation     and    rating   systems,    employment        analytics

dashboards, and mobile application development.  While these existing studies and systems

demonstrate the value of digital employment platforms in improving recruitment processes

and providing convenient access to employment opportunities, a critical examination of the

available   literature  reveals   significant   gaps   that  justify  the  development       of  the proposed

system.

         Existing employment platforms reviewed in this chapter — including At-Your-Service

(Basbas   et  al., 2019),   LocalLink    (Supo   et  al., 2024),   and   the  Smart   Employment Platform

(2025)   — address some        aspects   of  digital  employment matching          but are either     limited  in

scope,   focused    on   specific   worker    categories,    or  designed     without    the  combination      of

proximity-aware       job   allocation,    mutual     reputation      scoring,   worker     verification,    and

barangay-level     employment       analytics   that  the  proposed     system    integrates.   National-scale

platforms    and   corporate    recruitment     systems    reviewed    in  the international literature       are

similarly  not   designed    to  address    the informal hiring practices and         localized employment

challenges    specific  to  communities      like  Tayabas    City, where    domestic     helpers   and   skilled

workers continue to rely on word-of-mouth referrals and personal networks.

         The   absence     of   a  community-specific,        multi-feature     employment        platform    for

Tayabas   City  —   one   that   is accessible    through    both   web   and   mobile    channels,    supports

administrative worker       verification,   provides   localized   employment analytics, and promotes

mutual accountability through reputation scoring — demonstrates that the proposed system

addresses    a  genuine    and   unmet    need    in the   local employment       landscape. The proposed


<!-- Page 26 -->

system     fills this   gap   by    bringing    together     proximity-aware        smart    matching,      mutual

reputation scoring, worker         verification, barangay-level employment analytics, and a mobile

application    into   one    platform     that   is  specifically   built   for  the   employment        needs    of

households,     domestic     helpers,   and skilled workers in Tayabas City. Through the integration

of  these   technologies,     the   proposed      system    aims   to  provide     a  reliable,  organized,     and

inclusive   platform    that  connects     households     with   domestic     helpers   and skilled workers in

Tayabas    City,  filling  a  gap   that   existing   national    and    international     platforms     have   not

addressed.

Technical Background

         This section presents the technical specifications and tools used in the development

of the   proposed     GawaGo      Employment       Platform.    It covers   the  server-side    requirements,

client-side   requirements,      and   the   development       tools  employed      throughout     the   system

development      lifecycle.  The technologies      described in     this section were      selected   based   on

their  reliability,  compatibility     with   modern      web    and   mobile    application    development,

security   features,   and   overall   suitability  to  the  functional    requirements      of the   proposed

system.


 Server-Side Requirements

         The   server-side     of  GawaGo      handles     the   main    system    functions    such    as  user

authentication,      database     operations,      business     logic,  notifications,     and    employment

analytics. The technologies used were chosen for their reliability, security, and scalability.


    ●    Operating System - The development environment uses Windows 11, which provides

         a stable   platform for coding, testing, and debugging. For deployment, the system is

         intended to run on Ubuntu Server Linux, which is widely used for web hosting due to

         its stability, security, and performance.

    ●    Backend     Language     and    Framework       - The    system    uses   Python    as  the   backend

         language and Django as the framework. Python is chosen for its simplicity and strong

         support    for  web    development,       while   Django    provides    built-in   features   such    as

         authentication, database        management (ORM), and security protection. Its structure

         supports fast development and easy maintenance for multiple system modules.

    - Database Management System - The system uses Django ORM with MySQL as the target
      database management system. The database stores user accounts, profiles, job postings,
      applications, reviews, notifications, verification records, and related platform data.
      SQLite may be used only for local development or testing when needed.
    ●    API  Integration    Tools  - The system uses APIs mainly           for email notifications through

         Django’s email system. It sends alerts for registration, job updates, application status,

         and verification results. During development, emails are tested through the console,

         while   production      will  use   an   SMTP     email    service.   The    system    currently     uses

         rule-based logic for smart matching and does not rely on external AI APIs.


Client-Side Requirements

         The   client-side   component        of  the   system    provides    the   graphical    user   interface

through which households, workers, and administrators interact with the GawaGo platform.

The client-side technologies were selected to ensure a responsive, accessible, and consistent

user experience across desktop and mobile devices.


    - Mobile Frontend Technologies - The client-side application of the platform is developed
      using Flutter and the Dart programming language. Flutter allows the system to provide a
      cross-platform mobile interface for Android and future mobile deployment while maintaining
      a consistent user experience. The Flutter mobile application connects to the Django backend
      API to access account management, job posting, application, hiring, notification, verification,
      review, and analytics features.
